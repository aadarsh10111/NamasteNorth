Langtang National Park Trek Guide

Kyanjin Gompa, Gosaikunda & Tamang Culture

7-14 Days Moderate 3,870m Max

Close to Kathmandu yet wild and quiet, Langtang National Park mixes glaciers, rhododendron forests, holy lakes, and welcoming Tamang villages. The park spans alpine to subtropical zones and shelters rare wildlife like red panda and snow leopard—plus more than 250 bird species.

Why Choose Langtang?

Kyanjin Gompa

Lookout days with big views of Langtang Lirung and Tserko Ri (day hikes from the village). Kyanjin Gompa sits around 3,860–3,870 m, so plan to acclimatize.

Gosaikunda

A sacred cluster of alpine lakes within the park and a designated Ramsar wetland (2007). These holy lakes are a powerful cultural and spiritual destination.

Tamang Heritage

Villages where homestays and monasteries add real cultural depth to the trek. Experience authentic local culture and traditions.

Main Routes & Duration

Langtang offers three classic routes you can trek separately or combine:

  • Langtang Valley → Kyanjin Gompa: 6–10 days total from/to Kathmandu, with day hikes. The most popular route with stunning valley views.
  • Gosaikunda Lakes: 4–6 trekking days; can link via Lauribina La. Sacred lakes and spiritual significance.
  • Helambu: Gentler cultural route; can connect to Gosaikunda. Perfect for cultural immersion and shorter treks.

7-Day Sample Plan (Langtang Valley Focus)

Day 1: Drive Kathmandu → Syabrubesi (1,450 m)
Day 2: Syabrubesi → Lama Hotel (~2,480 m) - 6–7 hours
Day 3: Lama Hotel → Langtang Village (~3,430 m) - 6–7 hours
Day 4: Langtang → Kyanjin Gompa (~3,860–3,870 m) - 3–4 hours
Day 5: Tserko Ri Day Hike (Optional) - Panoramic views
Day 6: Kyanjin → Lama Hotel/Thyangsyap - Long descent
Day 7: Trek out to Syabrubesi; Drive to Kathmandu

Essential Information

Best Time

Autumn (Sep–Nov) for clearest skies, Spring (Mar–May) for rhododendron blooms

Permits Required

Langtang National Park Entry Permit + TIMS Registration

Difficulty

Moderate - suitable for most fitness levels with proper preparation

Accommodation

Teahouses throughout with basic facilities and local meals
